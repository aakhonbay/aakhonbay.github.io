Welcome back to the 2018-2019 school year! I am thrilled to begin yet another year here with you at King Abdullah Academy (KAA), an AdvancED accredited International Baccalaureate World School. Here at KAA, we work to nourish and develop active, compassionate, and lifelong learners through an educational community that fosters and embodies the IB learner profile. Our students are principled, open-minded, balanced, knowledgeable, caring, reflective, communicators, thinkers and inquirers, and risk-takers. We educate and raise our children to become well-rounded citizens of the world!
 
That said, this school year promises to be another exciting one! It continues to be my priority to ensure that my students have a safe and positive environment to learn and grow, and to make sure the quality of instruction is exponentially educational. As we implement our new Positive Behavior System (PBIS) initiative, we aim to implement and teach our students to the skills and tools necessary to be responsible, be respectful, and be ready, always.  My team and I will continue to participate in ongoing professional development and deliver the most current and relevant curriculum, instruction, and up-to-date technology to students.

A message from the Elementary Department: To ensure that all our students stay focused in a healthy and active environment, we kindly request that parents provide healthy snack and lunch options for our students. KAA follows a strict dietary guideline for our students, that we request you read through carefully: the only snacks allowed in our classrooms will include a fruit, vegetable, dairy, grains (if classroom refrigerator is available or the child has a chilled lunch box) or meat snack each day.  All snack times, grades K-5, will be lasting no more than 15 minutes.  Snack times have been worked into the classroom schedule by the teacher.  The only beverages allowed with snacks are water or milk. High fat, high carbs, and sugary foods will not be allowed. These items will be returned home if brought in (i.e. cookies, fruit snacks, fruit roll ups, snack cakes, chips, chocolate bars, candy). Please be mindful of allergies - NO nut products of any kind are allowed.  We are a NUT FREE school throughout every part of our school day, including on bus rides.  Due to health regulations, no food will be stored in the classroom and all uneaten opened snacks will be disposed of. Unopened snacks will be returned home. Please also keep in mind that a daily snack of individual portions is the responsibility of the child, as no snack will be provided by the school. Acceptable snacks include but may not be limited to: fruits or vegetables – cut into child size pieces, raisins, dried fruit, cottage cheese, yogurt, pretzels, popcorn, frozen grapes, dates, string cheese, apple sauce, lunch meat, cheese, crackers, peeled hard boiled eggs, olives, mini-pizzas, and mini-sandwiches.

A message from the Education Department: Our first couple of weeks will focus on introductions, expectations, diagnostic tests, and then we will jump into our content. Academic goals this year will focus on teaching our students important skills. Our attention this year will focus on our student’s learning methods. This will be achieved through focusing on our feedback cycle in the Middle Year Program (MYP 6th - 10th grade) and Diploma Program (DP 11th – 12th grade) programs. Our teaching philosophy is that we are teachers of learning, as well as teachers of content. The five skills we will focus on are: thinking, communication, social, self-management and research skills. These skills will become an integral part of every course, guiding our children to become developed active learners, positive members of our school community, and within our greater society. From K-12 King Abdullah Academy offers curriculum based on the IB, Common Core standards for Elementary (K-5), STEM elements, Next Generation Science Standards, and project-based learning.  To further support the implementation of these programs, learning communities in the school are arranged to promote engaged students and teachers. The IB approaches to teaching according to the IBO are based on: inquiry, conceptual understanding, developed in local and global context, focused on teamwork and collaboration, include differentiation to meet the needs of all learners, and are informed by formative and summative assessments. During the school year MAP and WIDA assessments to monitor student growth from grades 2-11 will occur during the Fall and Spring. IB Diploma examinations are schedule to begin in May. Educators at KAA will incorporate data-based decision making to impact growth in all students. The Academy also has an Academic Counselor, a new Special Needs Coordinator, and a Guidance counselor to aid in preparing students with the necessary social and academic skills needed to succeed in future education and the increasingly global 21st century. KAA also values physical fitness and overall health as we teach to the whole child. Our physical education department will focus on robust athletic programs including: soccer, volleyball, basketball, swimming, and dance to encourage students to lead healthy lifestyles. Our holistic approach at KAA provides an environment and structure where students have opportunities to grow and thrive.  
 
A message from the Nurse: KAA follows the rules and regulations of Fairfax County regarding health documentation and immunizations. To comply with Virginia Law, all new students are requested to provide the following items at the start of school: health form completed within the last 12 months prior to starting school, up-to-date vaccination records, and Tuberculosis screening. All returning students should make sure all immunizations and physicals are recent and up-to-date. If these forms have not already been submitted, please make sure that students bring the forms with them on the first day of school. It is important for the school to ensure the safety of students and ensure the school complies with local legal requirements. Students with incomplete enrollment packages will not be allowed to begin school on their intended start date. Any student in this situation will be required to commence later when the documentation is complete. Please note: Parents will be contacted by the nurse if student’s files are incomplete. This applies to both returning and newly enrolled students. Please also provide the nurse with any medications the students need to take at school with the doctor’s instructions on how to use these medications. Students will be given a Student Emergency Form on the first day of school. Parents are required to complete these forms and return them to school with the students as soon as possible. KAA’s Medical & health forms can be accessed at: http://www.kaa-herndon.com/admissions.html. To reach KAA’s School Nurse, please contact Ms. Denise Jalloh, RN, BSN, NCPT at Nurse@kaa-herndon.com

A message from Academic Counselor:  As the new school year 2018 – 19 begins, it is with great enthusiasm that we announce the establishment of KAA’s new Advisory Program. Through our new advisory program, we aim to support and complement our school’s mission and values. All students in grades 6 - 12 will be enrolled in an advisory group with 10 - 12 student peers. Each advisory group will be advised by two KAA teachers, that will provide each of student with individualized care and support as they navigate the academic and social challenges of our Middle Years and Diploma programs. During weekly group dialogue and impromptu individual conversations, our advisors will help students identify and cultivate their personal strengths and affinities. As a strengths-based program, a hosting of weekly meetings will help provide students with opportunities to discuss and reflect upon sets of skills, values, and experiences they will need for academic and personal success, within their school community, locally and globally. To learn more about the Advisory program, please contact KAA’s Academic Counselor, Anthony Riccio at ariccio@kaa-herndon.com
 
A message from the Guidance Counselor:  Our school offers counseling services as an additional resource to help your child succeed in their academic goals, as well as support social and emotional growth throughout the school year.  Counseling sessions in a school setting are typically short-term in nature and focus on solutions to help your child succeed in all areas of their development. Counseling activities can include individual or group counseling. The focus will be on helping students learn ways to build positive social skills, gain confidence in their ability to interact with others and learn ways to deal with problems and conflicts constructively. Counseling sessions typically meet once a week for thirty (30) minutes, at a time arranged with teachers.  Your student may be referred to counseling from a teacher, staff member or from you as a parent/guardian.  To make a referral, you may call the school or email. To reach the counseling office, please call the phone number 571-351-5520 Ext. 136, and/or email the School Guidance Counselor, Tassneem Al-Hanooti at Talhanooti@kaa-herndon.com
